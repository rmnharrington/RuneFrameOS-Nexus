Level 2 — Uncommon Ingredients
Uncommon ingredients that require some skill to acquire or prepare, found in specialized markets or requiring knowledge to harvest properly
Silverleaf Sage
(Salvia argentum — "The Tongue of the Moon")
A striking herb with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that glimmer with a metallic sheen, as though each surface were brushed with powdered silver.
Ashbloom Garlic
(Allium cinerea — "The Ember's Root")
Clusters of bulbs streaked with gray and charcoal hues, as though dusted in volcanic ash, with ivory-white cloves bearing faint smoke-colored veins.
Cindercress
(Flamma rubra — "The Emberleaf Herb")
A fiery herb with tiny scarlet-red fronds that resemble the glowing tips of embers, releasing faint heat when bruised and shimmering like captured sparks.
Whispering Anise
(Illicium susurris — "The Star of Secrets")
A star-shaped spice with eight pointed arms of deep brown wood, containing crystalline seeds that glimmer faintly and release a sweet, chiming sound when ground.
Ironroot Beet
(Beta ferrum — "The Rusted Heart")
A dense, heavy root with dark-red flesh veined in rusty brown streaks, bleeding deep crimson juice that stains with a rust-like patina when cut.
Twilight Onion
(Allium crepusculis — "The Dusk Bulb")
Pale, pearl-like bulbs with skins that shimmer faintly under fading light, their flesh glowing with gentle purple radiance when sliced, as though dusk itself were trapped within.
Mirepotato
(Solanum paludis — "The Swamp Root")
Bulky, mud-brown tubers with thick, ridged skins streaked with dark green and black stains from swamp soil, exuding a faint smoky aroma even before cooking.
Lanterncap Mushroom
(Agaricus aurorae — "The Amber Lantern")
Short, stout fungi with thick stems and wide, domed caps that glow with a gentle amber light in darkness, their smooth, leathery skin shading from pale cream to golden orange.
Bloodcitrus
(Citrus sanguinis — "The Scarlet Sun")
Medium-sized fruit with thick, dimpled skin ranging from deep orange to dusky crimson, revealing vivid scarlet flesh and juice that flows thick and dark like watered wine.
Glacier Berry
(Vaccinium glacialis — "The Frost's Gift")
Small, pale-blue fruits that grow in clusters along hardy alpine shrubs, their skins glistening as though dusted with frost and their icy-white flesh chilling the tongue with a faint numbing sensation.
Spicepeach
(Prunus ignis dulcis — "Firekissed Peach")
Plump, fuzzy orange fruits with a warm red blush on the sunward side, their velvety skins hiding golden, dripping flesh that releases a blossom-sweet perfume with gentle, peppered warmth.
Veinfruit
(Vitis sanguinea — "Heartcluster")
Tight, pendulous clusters of translucent crimson berries with fine, branching lines running through each berry like living filigree, carrying juice that beads at the stem when freshly cut.
Stormfowl Thigh
(Volucris tempesta — "The Thunderbird's Cut")
Dark, firm meat marbled with fine streaks of silver-veined fat, carrying a faint, prickling static that makes the skin rise in gooseflesh and an aroma that blends iron-rich game with the sharp tang of ozone.
Marrow Pike Steak
(Esox medulla — "The Bone-Rich Catch")
Formidable river fish with thick bodies and sharp teeth, their fatty, pale ivory flesh with faint gray shimmer releasing a rich mineral aroma and carrying a lingering marrow-like aftertaste.
Cinderhog Belly
(Sus cineris — "Ash-Born Pork")
Prized cut from thick-bodied boars raised in ash forests, their heavily marbled belly with alternating strata of soft fat and ruddy flesh releasing a faint campfire aroma even before cooking.
Moonhare Saddle
(Lepus lunaris — "The Night's Cut")
Tender loin from silver-furred nocturnal creatures that graze in moonlit glades, their lean, supple meat faintly iridescent when caught in dim light and holding a dusky aroma of damp grass and cold night air.
Verdant Oil
(Olea viridiflora — "Greenfire Press")
Pressed from the mossy seeds of creeping forest vines, carrying a luminous green hue that shimmers faintly when held to light, with a grassy and nutty aroma that clings to the air with woodland freshness.
Rosefire Wine
(Vinum rosaflamma — "The Ember's Bloom")
Radiant pink wine that shimmers like liquid dawn in the glass, releasing a heady bouquet of wild roses and faint smoke, with the first sip flowing smooth and sweet before blooming into lingering warmth.
Seafoam Cream
(Lac maritimus — "Foam of the Tides")
Drawn from gentle sea cows that graze on kelp beds along moonlit shores, this thick, opalescent white cream with faint bluish streaks froths naturally when churned, carrying the ocean's breath within.
Ashdew Vinegar
(Acetum cineris — "Mist of the Emberdew")
Pale and cloudy vinegar with a surface always slightly hazed as if mountain mist clung to it, brewed from dew collected at dawn from charred mountain herbs, carrying a sharp, herbal tang with faint smoke.
